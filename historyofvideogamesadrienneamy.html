History of Video Games

The Beginning (1950's)

  • 1952: "OXO" released, developed at the University of Cambridge
  • October 1958: First Video "Pong" released
  • 1959: "Mouse in the Maze", first game using a light pen

Video Games at Home (1960-1983)

  • 1975 "Gun Fight" Released in Japan, 2-player
  • 1972: "Magnavox Odyssey" was the first at-home console
  • 1972: The fist "Atari" was released in California and became a video-game icon

North American Video Game Crash (1983)

  • The Fairchild Channel F
  • Atari 2600
  • Intellivision

The Rise of Games in the East (1985-1995)

  • Nintendo Launches launches in 1985 in Japan
  • Games went from 16-bit to 32-bit
  • By 1995, Japan's "Sega" was also popular in Europe and the US

Tech Company Takeover (1995-2010)

  • The Power Macintosh 6200 was released in May 1995. It was released by Apple when many were switching to Windows
  • Microsoft was accused and tries for becoming a monopoly. They never broke up the company though they were found guilty of violating the Sherman Antitrust Act of 1890.
  • Zip drives were released by Iomega in 1994, which led the way for flash drives and CDs.

Today in Gaming (2010-now)

  • Online storefronts such as Wii Shop Channel and XBox Live Marketplace are mainstream
  • Mulitplayer long-distance gaming was popular by the time of the XBox360's release
  • Mobile gaming such as MarioKart are popular in 2019
  • The Rift headset by Oculus was released in 2016 and was a huge step in modern 3D VR gaming
  • Google bought Deep Mind in 2014 to advance and develop AI and further the gaming world
